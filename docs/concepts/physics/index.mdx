# Physics Foundations

This chapter keeps pure physics foundations separate from workflow-method pages. Use it for superconductivity, Josephson physics, circuit quantization, transmission-line intuition, and measurement physics. Use the other Concepts groups when the page is mainly about a research workflow method such as vector fitting, S/Y/Z trace interpretation, artifact contracts, or pulse-simulation placement.

The opening of each article will be a **Physics question sentence** or **Historical community context**, so that readers can immediately understand the important positioning of this article. The writing skeleton of each page is in order: opening, prior knowledge comparison, physics core (including symbol definition and formula derivation), engineering mapping, limitations and approximations, and cross-document navigation. The content is organized according to the A-I knowledge structure, from shallow to deep, and can be read linearly or cut into at will.

- All content follows [Physics Concepts Guardrail](../../reference/guardrails/documentation-design/explanation-physics.mdx)

</Aside>
